Core Island Services

Health, housing and emergency readiness.

This is the practical backbone: Elders, primary health, housing, aged care, police, ambulance, fire, marine rescue, wildlife rescue and volunteer safety.

Grant Notes

Different risk profile, different prep

  1. Health: confirm service authority, privacy, cultural safety, clinical governance and evidence of need.
  2. Housing: separate advocacy, tenancy support, repairs, crisis response, aged care and capital works.
  3. Emergency: distinguish public agencies, volunteer organisations, equipment, training, resilience and response coordination.
  4. Elders and culture: MMEIC and cultural authority must be treated as foundational, not decorative.
