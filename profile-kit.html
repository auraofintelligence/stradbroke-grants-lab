Profile Kit

Markdown packs for faster, cleaner grant drafting.

Start with the person, then connect the business, grant and noticeboard files only where they actually fit.

Individual Pathway

Person first.

These are the personal context files. They can stand alone for an individual, or become the human layer behind a business, artist practice, club role, committee role, grant applicant or noticeboard publisher.

Step 1

profile.md

Creates a public-facing personal profile: who someone is, where they belong, what they can offer, and what projects or community roles they want to be known for.

Use first when the person does not already have a current profile.md.

Step 2

aura.md

Creates deeper AI context for the person: intentions, values, tone, risks, preferences, planning context and reusable prompts.

Use after profile.md when the drafting needs personal context, creative direction or a clearer voice.

Already done?

Skip to business

If profile.md and aura.md already exist, move straight to the applicant and public notice files below.

This is the shortcut for returning clients, known artists, existing businesses and repeat grant work.

Business And Public Updates

Pair business-profile.md with noticeboard.md when public updates matter.

The business profile holds the applicant facts for grants. The noticeboard file holds the public update rules: what can be published, who approves it, and what should stay private.

Step 3

business-profile.md

Who the applicant is, what they do, ABN or entity notes, compliance, experience, evidence, community benefit and private boundaries.

Use for businesses, non-profits, clubs, artists, sole traders, auspiced projects and community groups.

Public companion

noticeboard.md

What is safe to publish, who approves updates, which notices are useful, and what should never be public by default.

Use beside business-profile.md when the applicant wants public updates, offers, events, grant milestones or community notices.

Next grant step

grant-readiness.md

Checks whether the applicant, project, timing, evidence, budget and permissions are ready for a specific grant.

Use once the person and applicant facts are clear.
